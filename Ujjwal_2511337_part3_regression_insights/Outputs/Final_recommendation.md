# Final Recommendation

The multiple regression model was selected as the final model because it gave the best results. It has an **R² value of 0.8570**, which means it explains about **85.7% of the changes in monthly sales**.

## Which factors are most strongly related to monthly sales?

The analysis shows that **footfall** and **staff count** have the strongest relationship with monthly sales. Marketing spend, holiday periods, and store type also have a positive effect on sales when all variables are considered together.

## Which variables should leadership focus on?

The company should mainly focus on:

* Increasing customer footfall.
* Maintaining enough staff in stores.
* Investing in effective marketing campaigns.
* Keeping inventory available so customers can find products easily.
* Planning special offers and staffing during holidays when sales are usually higher.

## Which variables should not be over-interpreted?

Variables such as **average discount**, **competitor distance**, and **customer rating** should not be judged on their own. They showed a weak relationship in the simple regression models, so decisions should not be based only on these factors.

## Business Recommendation

The company should focus on bringing more customers into stores because footfall has the strongest relationship with sales. It should also make sure stores have enough staff and products in stock. Marketing activities should continue, but their performance should be reviewed regularly to make sure the money is being spent effectively.

## Risks and Limitations

This model is based only on the available historical data. It does not include every factor that can affect sales, such as weather, local events, economic conditions, or competitor offers. Because of this, the model cannot predict sales perfectly for every situation.

## Why does regression not prove causation?

Regression only shows that two variables are related. It does not prove that one variable directly causes the other. For example, stores with higher footfall usually have higher sales, but this does not always mean that increasing footfall alone will increase sales. Other business factors may also affect the results. Therefore, regression results should be used along with business knowledge before making important decisions.
